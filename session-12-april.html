Inside Joel's Mind

Zone Offence — Be Aggressive First

For the first point, be as aggressive as you possibly can. If you shred a zone within three passes, there's a high chance you're not going to see it again — and it makes the whole defence react. If you're really patient and take 100-odd passes instead, the other team will think that was a win and play the same zone again.

I really like taking the first point aggressively the first time we see a zone, even if it doesn't matter. If you take on a big hammer over the top, the whole defence has to react — they know in their mind that's an option we might take on, and that's going to give us more space.

The second time we see a zone: just do the work. Take a hundred passes, put it away, and show that we're comfortable against it. That's the mindset every single time afterwards — it's going to take 100 passes, we take 100 passes, don't get greedy. Do the work and demonstrate to the defence that we're not scared of the zone.

Zone Offence — Know When to Slow Down

Recognise when we've burst through a zone and we're doing lots of quick passes — and then recognise when it's time to slow it down again and regain control. So many turns happen after taking one, two, three quick passes and then trying to force a fourth. We get a rush of blood to the head and go too quickly for our own good.

We want to gain yards, but we don't want to lose control. Slow it back down, regain control. You've done really well to gain some yardage — now keep the disc, do the hard work, and get the goal.

Zone Defence — Understand Your Purpose

The most important thing in zone D is understanding what the purpose of that zone point is. Is it to make them make lots of passes? To create pressure? To get aggressive poaches and blocks? That gives you a really good idea of how to play your role.

It's really important that you're not looking at the disc too much. You still need to keep an eye on it when it goes up, but you're more reacting off what players are doing — recognising who might be the next option and getting in position to stop it.

Zone Defence — Communication

Communication is critical. Everyone can talk and everyone is expected to talk. Deep talking to the wings, wings talking to the mid, mid talking into the hatchet, forces talking to the sideline — echoing all the calls, making lots of noise. Try to make it an intimidation chamber: almost adding another thing their team has to deal with on top of everything else.
